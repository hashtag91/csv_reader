Describe: generates a statistical summary of numeric columns in a data table.

Here are the key statistics provided by the function for numeric columns:

1. Count (count):

  • Description: Total number of non-zero (or non-missing) values ​​in the column.
  • Utility: Shows how much valid data is present in each column.

2. Mean (mean):

  • Description: Arithmetic mean of the values.
  • Utility: Measure of central tendency, indicating the average value of the data.

3. Standard Deviation (std):

  • Description: Standard deviation, which measures the dispersion or variability of values ​​relative to the mean.
  • Utility: Indicates how spread out the values ​​are around the mean.

4. Minimum (min):

  • Description: The smallest value in the column.
  • Utility: Provides an indication of the extent or range of the data.

5. Quartile 25% (25%):

  • Description: First quartile, also called lower quartile or Q1.
  • Utility: 25% of the data is less than this value. Used to understand the distribution of the data.

6. Quartile 50% (50%):

  • Description: Second quartile or median.
  • Utility: This is the midpoint of the data, 50% of the values ​​are lower and 50% are higher. Measure of central tendency robust to outliers.

7. Quartile 75% (75%):

  • Description: Third quartile, also called upper quartile or Q3.
  • Utility: 75% of the data is below this value. Measures the distribution of higher values.

8. Maximum (max):

  • Description: The largest value in the column.
  • Utility: Provides an idea of ​​the highest value in the data set.
